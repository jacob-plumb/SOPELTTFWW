SERIES OF POLITICAL EVENTS LEADING TO THE FIRST WORLD WAR

Holy Alliance, 1815

Prussia, Austria, and Russia joined together to combat the spread of republicanism in Europe and increase the influence of Christianity on European politics.

Source

Crimean War, 1853-65

The combined forces of Great Britain, France, and the Ottoman Empire defeat Russia over disputes of territorial expansion, dissolving the Holy Alliance.

League of Three Emperors, 1872

The kaiser of the newly unified German Empire renewed his old alliance with Russia and Austria-Hungary.

Dual Alliance, 1879

Russia and Austria-Hungary could not resolve the issue of influence in the Balkans, so Russia left the accord.

Triple Alliance, 1882

Italy has a territorial dispute with France and joins the Dual Alliance.

Franco-Russian Alliance, 1892

In response to the formation of the Triple Alliance, Russia and France sought to counterract it.

Entente Cordiale, 1904

A series of agreements between France and Great Britain.

Anglo-Russian Convention, 1907

An agreement between Russia and Great Britain which, in essence, created an unofficial alliance between France, Great Britain, and Russia called the Triple Entente.
